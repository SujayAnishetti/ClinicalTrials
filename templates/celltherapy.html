What is Cell Therapy?

Cell therapy represents a revolutionary approach to cancer treatment, using genetically modified immune cells to target and destroy cancer cells with unprecedented precision.

CAR-T Technology

Chimeric Antigen Receptor T-cell therapy uses your own immune cells, modified to better recognize and attack cancer cells.

Targeted Treatment

Specifically designed to target GPC3-positive tumors in advanced hepatocellular carcinoma with precision.

Personalized Medicine

Each treatment is customized using the patient's own cells, offering a personalized approach to cancer therapy.

Cell Therapy Eligibility

  • Age 18-65 years for most cell therapy trials
  • Specific cancer diagnosis matching trial criteria (e.g., Hepatocellular Carcinoma)
  • Previous treatment history as specified by the protocol
  • Adequate organ function to safely receive cell therapy
  • Performance status sufficient for participation

Important Information

Cell therapy trials have specific eligibility criteria that may differ from other cancer treatments. A thorough medical evaluation is required to determine suitability.

Note: Final eligibility is determined by the study team after comprehensive medical review. Expressing interest does not guarantee participation.
